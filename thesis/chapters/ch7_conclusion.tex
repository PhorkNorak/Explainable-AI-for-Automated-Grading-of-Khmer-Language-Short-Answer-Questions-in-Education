\chapter{Conclusion}
\label{ch:conclusion}

This thesis set out to build the first reproducible, \emph{explainable} automatic short-answer
grading benchmark for Khmer, and to characterise honestly what current model families can achieve on
it. It delivered a 1{,}184-answer teacher-graded corpus with three curated variants, a Khmer-aware
preprocessing pipeline, a unified pipeline comparing four model families (classical, RNN, Transformer,
and a fine-tuned LLM), a cross-family faithfulness study, a quantified question-leakage analysis, and
a live teacher-facing prototype.

The six research questions are answered as follows. \textbf{(RQ1)} All four pillars reach QWK in the
0.80--0.85 range, and none is universally best. \textbf{(RQ2)} Data cleaning lifts the same model by
$+0.027$ QWK, at least as much as swapping architectures on this small corpus. \textbf{(RQ3)} On QWK
the pillars are comparable (a 0.05 band), and the fine-tuned LLM is the clear winner only on
the deployment metrics (67\% exact match, 79\% within one point). \textbf{(RQ4)} The max-score feature
gives consistent, near-free gains, while post-hoc calibration is fragile and model-dependent and so is
reported as an ablation rather than in the headline. \textbf{(RQ5)} Leave-One-Out (LOO) occlusion word attribution is reliably faithful for every model
family across all four pillars, as verified by ERASER comprehensiveness and sufficiency. Explanations
have to be \emph{measured}. \textbf{(RQ6)} Under a question-held-out split the
classical QWK collapses from 0.76 to 0.35, showing that small-question-pool ASAG scores are heavily
inflated by question leakage.

The central message is that for Khmer short-answer grading, accuracy, deployment quality, and
explainability must be judged \emph{together}: a 30-second CPU classical model is competitive on the
research metric and explains itself faithfully through word attribution, a fine-tuned LLM is the right choice
when exact teacher scores matter, and, above all, an explanation is only worth showing to a teacher
if it has been shown to be faithful. By releasing the dataset, code, seeds, and results, and by
realising the work as a usable prototype, this thesis provides both a foundation for future Khmer
educational NLP and a cautionary, reusable lesson about evaluating grading systems honestly.
